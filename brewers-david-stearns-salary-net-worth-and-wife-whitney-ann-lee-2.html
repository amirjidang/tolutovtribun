    Milwaukee Brewers: David Stearns Net Worth And Salary

    David Stearns has an estimated net worth of $5 million and a salary of $210,786 per year. According to Comparably.com, the average MLB executive compensation is $210,786.

    Stearns joined the Brewers after serving as the Houston Astros’ assistant general manager for three seasons, where he was an important management group member. He concentrated on all elements of baseball operations, such as analytics, management, player development, and scouting.

    Stearns served as the Cleveland Indians’ director of baseball operations for a year, from December 2011 to November 2012. He concentrated on contract negotiations, payroll analysis, organizational strategy, and roster management.

    Stearns then left the Indians to join the Astros. He served as the manager of labor relations at the Office of the Commissioner from 2008 to 2011, where he helped with the pay arbitration procedure, dealt with contracts for uniformed players, and was a part of Major League Baseball’s negotiating team for the Collective Bargaining Agreement.

    The Brewers appointed Matt Arnold as general manager following the 2020 campaign, holding Stearns in his position as president of baseball operations.

    David Stearns Was Assistant General Manager Of Houston Astros

    David Stearns worked as the Houston Astros’ assistant general manager for three seasons, from 2013 to 2015 before joining the Milwaukee Brewers.

    The Astros experienced one of the most notable recent turnarounds in Major League Baseball during his time in Houston, rising from 51 wins in 2013 to 70 wins in 2014.

    The Houston Astros hired Stearns as their assistant general manager in November 2012 despite their over 100-game losing streaks over the previous two seasons.

    Stearns completed internships in baseball operations with the New York Mets and the Pittsburgh Pirates. In 2007, he served as the Arizona Fall League’s deputy director of baseball operations.

    David Was Named Milwaukee Brewers General Manager

    David Stearns was appointed the Milwaukee Brewers’ new general manager on September 21, 2015. Stearns changed half of the players on the Brewers’ 40-man roster during his first offseason in that position.

    In his initial deals, he traded for Jonathan Lucroy and bought Anthony Swarzak, Travis Shaw, and Eric Thames. Stearns received a contract extension from the Brewers in January 2019 and was elevated to the positions of general manager and president of baseball operations.

    He remained the Brewers’ president of baseball operations following the 2020 season, and Matt Arnold was elevated to general manager.

    David Step Downs As Brewers President

    The Brewers announced on October 27, 2022, that Stearns would quit his position as president of baseball operations and accept an advising position with ownership.

    Matt Arnold, the general manager, would assume control of baseball operations. The decision was made following a poor season for the Brewers, during which the team missed the playoffs for the first time in five years.

    Josh Hader, the team’s outstanding closer, was traded on August 1, 2022, one day before the trading deadline. Milwaukee was leading the NL Central at the time but would end the year seven games behind the St. Louis Cardinals.

    Stearns was seen highly by many MLB teams. In the past, the New York Mets were listed as a team that was interested in his services, but Stearns stated that he is now not really interested in working in the front office of another team.

    He was also in charge of executing the deal that sent Christian Yelich from Miami to Milwaukee. When the Brewers made it to the NL Championship Series in 2018, Yelich was voted the NL MVP.
