    SHRA Non-Exempt and Exempt Employees

    The University Of North Carolina System employs both SHRA non-exempt and SHRA exempt employees. These distinctions determine the eligibility for overtime compensation and play a significant role in the university’s pay scale.

    SHRA Non-Exempt Employees:

    Non-exempt employees within the University Of North Carolina System are entitled to receive overtime pay for hours worked over 40 in a single work week. This additional compensation ensures that employees are fairly remunerated for their extra time and effort.

    SHRA Exempt Employees:

    In contrast, SHRA exempt employees are not eligible for overtime pay. These employees receive a fixed salary regardless of the hours worked beyond the standard 40-hour workweek. The exemption status for these employees is determined based on an evaluation of their job duties and responsibilities.

    Please note that the University Of North Carolina System follows federal and state regulations regarding overtime pay and exempt status for its employees.

    SHRA ExemptNot eligible for overtime pay

    SHRA Non-Exempt Part-Time Employees

    Within the University Of North Carolina System, there are part-time employees who fall under the SHRA non-exempt category. These employees, while working part-time hours, still have certain rights and benefits, including the accrual of “gap hours” for any time worked between their regular scheduled hours and 40 hours.

    It is important to note that these “gap hours” are compensated at the regular hourly rate of the employee.

    SHRA Exempt Employees

    SHRA exempt employees within the University Of North Carolina System are not eligible for time-and-a-half compensation for overtime hours. The exemption status is determined based on an evaluation of job duties and responsibilities.

    “Exemption status is assigned to employees who meet specific criteria and perform certain job duties. These employees are considered exempt from the overtime provisions of the Fair Labor Standards Act (FLSA).”

    Exempt employees in the UNC System are typically classified as executive, administrative, or professional staff. This designation is based on their job responsibilities, level of decision-making authority, and primary duties.

    Exempt employees are expected to fulfill their job duties and responsibilities, regardless of the number of hours worked. They receive a fixed salary and are not eligible for overtime compensation, regardless of the number of hours worked beyond their regular schedule.
