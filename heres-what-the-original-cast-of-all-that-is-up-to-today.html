    If you grew up in the '90s, chances are you're familiar with Nickelodeon's sketch comedy All That. Hailed as one of the longest-running and most diverse Nickelodeon series of all time, All That's first six seasons were something special. Not only did it launch the careers of teen actors like Amanda Bynes, but those early cast members made up the original blueprint of All That that shaped the Nickelodeon culture into what we know today.

    Related: 10 Celebs Who Guest Starred On Nickelodeon

    Now, many of the show's original cast has ventured of into other things. Unfortunately, for every star that broke into Hollywood, there are others who fell away from the fame. To sum things up, here's what the original cast of All That has been up to since the show ended.

    10 Christy Knowings

    Christy Knowings replaced Alisa Reyes as one of the regulars of All That in seasons four, five, and six. These days, the Bronx actress seems to be enjoying some time away from Hollywood.

    In addition to, All That, Knowings appeared in several episodes of Sesame Street from 2008 to 2011. Plus, she's just released her gritty folk single, "To the World," in December 2020.

    9 Leon Frierson

    Leon Frierson joined the cast of All That along with Christy Knowings. He held the position from the fourth season to the sixth season before coming as a guest star in season ten. Now, Frierson is a podcast host of Prime Nostalgia, which you can listen to on Spotify and Apple Music. He also has an online store of the same name that sells old-age merchandise from the golden age of pop culture.

    8 Amanda Bynes

    After All That, Amanda Bynes received her own Nickelodeon skit show, The Amanda Show, which ran for three years. Bynes then went on to star in the sitcom What I Like About You and a few popular big-screen movies, including Easy A and She's The Man. However, not everyone can cope with the sudden amount of fame at a young age, and unfortunately, some of stars fall into the black trap of substance abuse.

    Related: 10 Former Nickelodeon Actors Who Live Life Off-Screen Now

    Amanda Bynes's struggle with her addiction has been well-documented throughout the years. Fortunately, she has sought sobriety and has been sober for a few years.  She is now in the healthiest stage she could have ever been and is ready to pick up what she left off from her career.

    7 Kenan Thompson

    Undoubtedly one of the most successful All That alum, Kenan Thompson has served as a cast member of Saturday Night Live since departing Nickelodeon. It made him one of the longest-serving cast in the history of television.

    Now, the talented comedian has several works on his horizon. His latest comedy project, Kenan, was just released on NBC in February 2021.

    6 Josh Server

    Josh Server was only 15 when he tapped the All That pilot episode. Little did he know, he would soon become the only cast member to remain through all the six original seasons.

    As for now, after departing Nickelodeon, Server seems to be enjoying smaller roles. He made some All That reunion-type cameos, including in a 2018 episode of Nick Cannon's Wild 'N Out and Double Dare.

    5 Alisa Reyes

    After departing All That, Alisa Reyes continued to elevate her career to new levels. She made her move into Disney and starred in The Proud Family from 2001 until 2005 as LaCienega Boulevardez. She also is a versatile entertainer, having worked as a DJ for Playboy Radio on Sirius and XM Satellite Radio.

    Now, the 40-year-old actress is expected to reprise her role on Disney+ upcoming reboot of The Proud Family, which will air later this year.

    4 Kel Mitchell

    It's not surprising that Kel Mitchell, who played some of All That's most memorable characters, continued to find success after leaving the show. After his stint on the show came to an end, Mitchell continued to serve as one of the two titular protagonists on Nickelodeon's Kenan & Kel comedic series, with Kenan Thompson.

    Related: Kenan Thompson Finally Has His Own Show, And '90s Kids Can't Wait

    Since the '90s, Mitchell has found success in voice acting and is even reprising some of his old characters for Nick's revamped version of All That. The 42-year-old stand-up comedian is also trying out his hand with talent shows, as he's gearing up to join the judging panel of MTV's Ridiculousness spin-off, Deliciousness.

    3 Katrina Johnson

    Katrina Johnson from All That is now all grown up. She served as one of the main characters in the show's first three seasons.

    Despite enjoying such a long hiatus from Hollywood in the past decade, Johnson also ventured into radio presenting and stand-up comedy. Her latest radio show, Uncensored Radio's Guilty Pleasures, aired in 2015.

    2 Lori Beth Denberg

    Another well-known All That cast member, Lori Beth Denberg has since been recognized for her work in The Steve Harvey Show from the third to sixth season. During her time with Nickelodeon, she also served in the judging panel of the Figure It Out competition show.

    Last year, as the actress told Vice, she did a Nostalgia Personified tour with former All That co-star Danny Tamberelli around California to have friendly chats and beers with Nickelodeon fans across the U.S. Denberg has also made a few reappearances on Nick's new All That.

    1 Angelique Bates

    Angelique Bates' short-lived career with Nickelodeon only lasted two seasons before the actress departed the All That. Since then, she hasn't been in a major project except several small cameos here and there, including in Strong Medicine and Fox's Boston Public.

    However, she does have some excellent work in her underrated musical discography. Besides appearing in Michael Jackson's "Love Never Felt So Good" music video, Bates also released her debut international single, "Can't Fade Me Now," back in 2012.
