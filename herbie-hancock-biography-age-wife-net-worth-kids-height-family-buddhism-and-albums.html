    Herbie Hancock Biography/ Wiki

    Herbie Hancock was born as Herbert Jeffrey Hancock on April 12, 1940. He is an American pianist, keyboardist, bandleader, composer, and actor. Hancock achieved success as an incisive, harmonically provocative jazz pianist and then went on to gain wide popularity as a leader of electric jazz-rock groups.

    Herbie Hancock Education

    Hancock attended Hyde Park High School, a public 4–year high school established in the Woodlawn neighborhood on the south side of Chicago, Illinois. He studied classical music from the age of 7 and his talent was recognized from his early age of 5.

    Herbie attended Grinnell College where he graduated with degrees in electrical engineering and music, The American composer then moved to Chicago where he took classes at Roosevelt University. Hancock also learned at Manhattan School of Music in New York.

    Herbie Hancock Parents

    Hancock was born in Chicago, IL, the son of Winnie Hancock, a secretary, and Wayman Edward Hancock (died 2001), a government meat inspector. His parents choose his name after the American actor of film and television and popular music and jazz singer-songwriter Herb Jeffries. He was raised in a family of 4 children, Herbie has a brother and two sisters, Wayman Hancock, Jr., Jean Hancock and Earline Greenfield.

    Hancock practices Nichiren Buddhism, a practice he adopted in 1972. He is a part of the Buddhist association Soka Gakkai International. As part of Hancock’s spiritual tradition, he recites the Buddhist mantra Nam Myoho Renge Kyo daily.
