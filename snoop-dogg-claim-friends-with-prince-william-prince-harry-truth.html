The brothers have hung out with everybody in the showbiz industry it seems, from James Corden to Oprah Winfrey. So what's the truth behind Snoop Dogg's claim that he's buddies with the princes?

    6 Things Started Way Back in 2010 Between Snoop And The Princes

    According to Vanity Fair, the royal brothers' acquaintance with Snoop Dogg began back in 2010, when Harry contacted the rapper to ask if he would perform at brother William's bachelor party - just before his marriage. Snoop couldn't make the event, but a friendship began between them and numbers were exchanged.

    Shortly afterwards, he made a homage to his royal friends in the music video for his track 'Wet', which featured lookalikes of the royals. He said the song would be perfect for a 'stag do' in a tweet about the track. William and Harry evidently appreciated the jokey tribute, which cemented their friendship.

    5 Snoop Dogg Knew The Brothers Were Fans

    Snoop Dogg has also said that he reached out to William and Harry after hearing that they were fans, and said that he is supportive of the pair in a big-brother type way - looking out for them and calmly keeping an eye on happenings.

    “Harry and William are my boys,” Snoop Dogg told the Mirror this week. “As soon as I knew they were fans I reached out to them, and we’ve been cool ever since. I look out for them and they can reach out to me whenever they want they know that. I look out for them, and they can reach out to me whenever they want.”

    4 And Says His Music 'Raised Them'

    The rapper says that the brothers were not only familiar with his music, but were in fact huge fans, and had been listening to his music since their youth. He claims his music had a formative effect on the youngsters - and said it helped them find their sometimes rebellious spirit.

    In an interview with the Guardian, he said “I wanna shoot a shout-out to my homeboys in London: the princes, Harry and William" and went on to say that they had been fans since they were kids, "You know I raised them on this music of mine. They were groomed on me,” he said. “I’m a part of their revolt. They were at that age when my music spoke to them.”
