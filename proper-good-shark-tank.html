    What is Proper Good Soup? Who is the Founder of Proper Good Soup?

    Proper Good is a company that specializes in producing microwavable food pouches, offering a convenient solution for busy individuals who crave nutritious meals but lack the time to prepare them. The founders and current owners of Proper Good are Chris and Jennifer Jane, individuals with a strong business background. Chris, who attended Stanford Business School, brings his expertise in entrepreneurship and strategic management to the table, while Jennifer holds a marketing degree, contributing her marketing acumen to the company’s success.

    Their flagship product, Proper Good Soup, is a game-changer in the microwavable food industry. Unlike other options on the market, their soups can be heated and enjoyed in just 90 seconds, providing a quick and effortless meal solution. The convenient microwavable food pouches are made of transparent plastic, allowing customers to see the high-quality, natural ingredients inside. Additionally, Proper Good soups do not require refrigeration, making them perfect for people on the go.

    Proper Good caters to a variety of dietary needs, offering a range of soup options, including keto, dairy-free, gluten-free, and plant-based alternatives. This ensures that customers with specific dietary restrictions can enjoy a delicious and nourishing meal that meets their individual needs.

    “Our goal with Proper Good Soup is to provide busy individuals with a convenient and healthy meal option that doesn’t compromise on taste or quality. We understand the struggle of finding time to prepare nutritious meals, and that’s why we created Proper Good Soup – to give people an easy and satisfying solution.”

    What happened to Proper Good Soup at Shark Tank pitch?

    Proper Good Soup had an exciting appearance on Shark Tank in Season 13, Episode 2. The founders, Chris and Jennifer Jane, showcased their innovative home-delivered soup business to the panel of Sharks, hoping to secure an investment and take their company to new heights. Let’s take a closer look at what transpired during their Shark Tank pitch.

    Dressed in their Proper Good Soup aprons, Chris and Jennifer Jane confidently presented their microwavable, ready-to-eat soup pouches to the Sharks. They offered the Sharks a taste of their delicious and nutritious soups, prepared in just 90 seconds.

    The Sharks were impressed with the quality and convenience of Proper Good Soup. Mark Cuban, Barbara Corcoran, and the other Sharks sampled the different flavors and expressed their approval. The Jane siblings had captured their attention.

    “I can see this becoming a staple in people’s pantries. The flavors are fantastic!” – Mark Cuban

    The founders had clearly done their research and came prepared with their financials. Chris and Jennifer Jane asked for a $400,000 investment in exchange for a 10% stake in their company, valuing Proper Good Soup at $4 million. They presented their sales figures and growth potential, showcasing their ambition and dedication to success.

    However, Mark Cuban saw even greater potential in Proper Good Soup and decided to make an offer. He offered $400,000 for a 25% stake in the company, indicating his confidence in its future prospects. The siblings countered with a 20% stake, striking a deal that they believed would be mutually beneficial.

    “I’m willing to invest in your business because I believe in the product and your vision. Let’s make Proper Good Soup a household name!” – Mark Cuban

    The Jane siblings successfully secured the investment from Mark Cuban, a Shark known for his business acumen and success. This partnership would provide Proper Good Soup with the financial backing and expertise needed to expand their operations and reach a wider audience.

    What happened to Proper Good Soup after Shark Tank?

    After appearing on Shark Tank, Proper Good Soup experienced a significant sales boost, selling three and a half months’ worth of soup in just one week. This exceptional surge in demand was a testament to the quality and appeal of their microwavable soups.

    With their innovative product and newfound exposure, Proper Good Soup was able to generate a revenue of $2 million in 2021. Their success can be attributed to their direct-to-consumer model, which allows them to reach a wide audience and provide convenient and nutritious meals.

    Looking forward, Proper Good Soup has expressed interest in expanding their reach into retail. By partnering with stores and supermarkets, they aim to make their products more accessible to a larger customer base.     About the Founders of Proper Good Soup

    The founders of Proper Good Soup, Chris and Jennifer Jane, are sibling entrepreneurs who share a passion for providing convenient, healthy, and delicious meals for busy individuals. Chris Jane serves as the CEO and Co-Founder of Proper Good, drawing on his extensive experience in business, marketing, and entrepreneurship. Jennifer Jane, the Founder & Chief Marketing Officer (CMO), brings her expertise in marketing to the company’s strategic growth plans.

    Chris and Jennifer established Proper Good with the vision of revolutionizing the way people consume soup. They wanted to create a product that not only offered convenience but also prioritized high-quality ingredients. By developing microwavable food pouches that can be heated and enjoyed in just 90 seconds, they have succeeded in providing a convenient and nutritious solution for those on the go.

    Committed to meeting the diverse dietary needs of their customers, Chris and Jennifer offer a range of dietary alternatives, including keto, dairy-free, gluten-free, and plant-based options. This dedication to inclusivity and customer satisfaction has contributed to Proper Good Soup’s popularity and success in the market.

    With their innovative approach to soup and a clear understanding of consumers’ needs, Chris and Jennifer Jane continue to drive Proper Good Soup towards new levels of growth and convenience.

    Philanthropic Efforts of Proper Good Soup

    Proper Good Soup goes beyond just providing delicious and nutritious meals. The company is dedicated to making a positive impact on communities facing food deserts. Understanding the challenges faced by individuals living in areas with limited access to affordable, healthy food, Proper Good Soup takes action by delivering nutritional meals directly to customers residing in food deserts.

    But Proper Good Soup doesn’t stop there. They also work with occupational food deserts, where access to healthy meals is often a challenge. By partnering with organizations in these areas, Proper Good Soup ensures that even individuals with demanding jobs have access to the nourishment they need to thrive.

    As part of their commitment to giving back, Proper Good Soup donates meals to charity for every social media post that tags them. Their support has already made a significant impact, with over 15,000 meals donated to Welfare, a New York-based nonprofit that assists food-insecure families. By combining their culinary expertise with philanthropy, Proper Good Soup is making a tangible difference in the lives of those in need.
