    Fans of Will & Grace know that Will Truman and Grace Adler are best friends on screen, but Debra Messing's Grace and Sean Hayes' Jack get along quite well, too. As die-hard fans know, Hayes used to be best friends with Megan Mullally, who played Karen. With the feud between Messing and her co-star, Mullally, fans may be wondering if Hayes and Messing are close in real life. It turns out they are!

    Related: Are 'Will & Grace' Stars, Debra Messing and Megan Mullally Friends?

    From making a guest appearance on Hayes' Hypochondriactor podcast to taking Buzzfeed's BFF test, these two are great friends in real life, despite any drama that occurred onset between the two female co-stars. The two have great love and respect for one another and have quite a bit in common, from various health issues to love for Broadway. Let's take a look at Hayes and Messing's wonderful friendship.

    7 Debra Messing Appeared On Sean Hayes' Podcast

    Messing appeared on Hayes' podcast, Hypochondriactor, where the two chatted about Messing's various ailments (both real and fake) over the years. It was on this podcast that fans may have realized just how close these two former co-stars are. They apparently text each other all the time and have commiserated over each other's health struggles over the years.

    6 Debra Messing And Sean Hayes Took Buzzfeed's BFF Test

    Hayes and Messing did the Buzzfeed BFF test on YouTube. One of the questions on the test was "what is one thing Sean can't live without?" Hayes answered with "Scotty", who is his husband, and Messing answered with "me" and faked crying when she discovered Hayes didn't put her as his answer. Hayes laughed and said "that was a good answer, which is true. Both people," he said. Besides that, their answers were pretty much the same for all of the questions, proving the two know each other very well after working together for so many years. At the end of the test, Messing very sweetly said, "I knew that Sean was my brother, but through this game, I learned that he really is my best friend." Hayes then responded and said, "I learned that because she just said that, I would probably sleep with her. I also learned that that's probably not true. But I also wanna say I learned nothing because I already know everything about her." So sweet!

    5 Debra Messing Supported Sean Hayes On Broadway

    On the opening night red carpet for Hayes' run on Broadway in An Act of God, Messing told Theater Mania that Hayes was like "her brother" and that he made her laugh "more than anyone in the world." She went on to say that "he really is touched with comic genius." The two friends posed for photos together at the after-party, as well. Messing has always loved Broadway and the theater and studied theater in college.

    4 Sean Hayes Noticed When Debra Messing Stopped Smoking

    On the Hypochondriactor podcast, Messing spoke about what finally made her quit smoking. It was when she got pregnant with her son, Roman. Hayes recalls weirdly catching Messing on the set of Will & Grace no longer smoking, and he asked her what was going on. She tried to play it off like it was nothing out of the ordinary but then revealed to him that she was pregnant. Before her pregnancy, she relied on smoking to help with her anxiety.

    3 Sean Hayes Was Sick During Debra Messing's Star On The Walk Of Fame Ceremony

    Messing received her star on the walk of fame in October 2017. Messing mentioned at the ceremony that her Will & Grace co-star Hayes was "under the weather", and that was the reason why he couldn't be there, but that she missed him very much, according to Today. She went on to say that Mullally and McCormack were here "brother and sister, my husband and wife -- my family. They are everything to me." For those who are familiar with Hayes' Hypochondriactor podcast, they are aware of how often the actor gets sick.

    2 Sean Hayes Guest Starred On Debra Messing's Show 'Smash'

    While the two former co-stars didn't have any scenes together on the NBC series, Smash, Hayes did a multiple-episode arc on Messing's series. Hayes played the character of Terrence Falls, who, according to TVLine, was a "comedic television and film star who is making his Broadway debut in the musical Liaisons, based on the novel Les Liaisons Dangereuses. Due to a series of comic circumstances, he becomes a major thorn in the side for Ivy Lynn and other characters." It's a bummer the two friends didn't have any scenes together, though.

    1 Sean Hayes Pinched Debra Messing's Butt At Every Will & Grace Curtain Call

    For those who have been to a live taping of Will & Grace or have seen clips from tapings, they will know that Hayes always pinched Messing's butt when she and Eric McCormack took their bows during the curtain calls. Mullally simultaneously would pinch McCormack's butt during each curtain call. The two would have to be pretty close in order for Hayes to do that, right? The tradition ran through the first run of the series and continued on through the reboot.
