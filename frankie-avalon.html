    Frankie Avalon Biography Timeline

    Frankie Avalon was an American actor, singer, and former teen idol born in Philadelphia, Pennsylvania, on September 18, 1940. With a career spanning several decades, he rose to fame in the 1950s and 1960s as a pop singer and later as an actor in various musical films. Throughout his career, Avalon has released numerous hit songs and starred in numerous popular films. Here is a timeline of his life and career.

    1940: Born in Philadelphia, Pennsylvania.

    1955: Signed a record contract with Chancellor Records.

    1956: “De De Dinah” becomes his first charting single.

    1957: Releases his first album, “Frankie Avalon”.

    1959: Co-starred with Annette Funicello in the film “Beach Party”.

    1960: Released the hit single “Why”, which reached No.1 on the US charts.

    1962: Appeared in several musical films, including “Boys’ Night Out”, “Drums of Africa”, and “Beach Party”.

    1963: He released the hit single “Venus”, his biggest hit, reaching No.1 on the US charts.

    1969: Retired from singing and focused on acting.

    1978: Released the album “Frankie Avalon” and embarked on a successful tour.

    1982: Appeared in the film “The Return of Frankie Avalon”.

    1998: Inducted into the Philadelphia Music Walk of Fame.

    2010: Published his memoir “Boy to Man: My Life from Childhood to Mainstream”.

    Frankie Avalon was a famous singer and actor in the 1950s and 1960s. He rose to fame with several hit songs, including “Venus”, and his appearance in various musical films. Throughout his career, he has sold millions of records and remained a beloved figure in popular culture.
