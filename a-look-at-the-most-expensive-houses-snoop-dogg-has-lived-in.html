    Snoop Dogg has earned a staggering net worth that he spends on buying expensive luxuries, including multiple real estate properties in California that cost more than a few million dollars.

    Cordozar Calvin Broadus was nicknamed Snoopy by his stepfather as the young boy loved the animated character from the show Peanuts. He developed a love for music while playing the piano and singing in the church choir and worked odd jobs as a teenager. Using the stage name Snoop Dogg, he released a mixtape Hold On that landed with Dr. Dre, signed him up with his record label Aftermath Entertainment and released his debut album, Doggystyle, which topped the Billboard charts. Snoop Dogg has amassed a whopping $150 million net worth through his music, record label, and movie cameos. Along with purchasing cars and jewelry, the rapper has spent millions buying homes.

    Snoop Dogg purchased several properties early in his career around California and renovated them with the changing time. As one of the biggest rappers in the world, his mansions are just as larger than life. Let’s look at the most expensive houses Snoop Dogg has lived in.

    Claremont, California Home

    After being discovered by Dr. Dre in 1992, Snoop Dogg’s career took off, making him a chart-topping rapper. Two years later, he purchased his first big mansion, a Mediterranean-style home, for $660,000. His house was first featured in MTV Cribs in 2000 and went through a renovation in 2006. The home was 3,700 square feet in size. However, the rapper extended the area to 6,527 square feet to accommodate the other rooms better. Located atop a hill, at the far end of a cul-de-sac, the estate is a few miles east of Los Angeles.

    According to Celebrity Net Worth, the home initially featured five bedrooms, but Snoop extended the house to accommodate eight bedrooms. The two-story home has dual Master Suites with attached walk-in closets. Other amenities in the home include marble fireplaces, a theater room, a pool with a pool house, a tennis court, and a recording studio. The home is structured with high ceilings, recessed lighting, crown moldings, and dual-pane windows. Additionally, the kitchen is a Chef’s dream with marble countertops and top-of-the-line steel appliances for use. After living in the home for a decade and renovating it, Snoop put the house on sale in 2007 and sold it for a profit, earning $2 million from the deal.

    Diamond Bar, California Mansion

    In 1998, Snoop Dogg decided to add another great property to his name, a mansion in Diamond Bar, one of the most expensive cities in California. While the rapper is open about sharing details about himself, he has been relatively secretive about giving fans a glimpse inside his estate. Snoop purchased the home for a bargain price of $720,000, while the house is estimated to be worth $1.7 million today, as noted by Hello Magazine. The 2.8 acres of land has the primary residence on 4,000 square feet of living space.

    The mansion houses four bedrooms and four bathrooms in County Estates, a gated community with top-rated security. Along with the basic amenities, his house also features a spa. Outdoors, his home is just as majestic. Snoop has a swimming pool, a pool house, a gazebo, and a basketball court. Palm trees and shrubbery shroud the large backyard of the traditional home to offer optimum privacy to Snoop and his family. Diamond Bar remains his primary residence as the rapper continues to live there and even gives small glimpses through his social media accounts.

    The Church

    Located on the same street as his dream-like mansion in Diamond Bar, Snoop Dogg purchased a modest three-bedroom a few years after buying his home, as stated by The Things. While it may seem odd that the rapper bought a house a few minutes from his primary residence, many were surprised when he transformed the mansion into a fully-functioning recording studio. Calling it his Church, Snoop spends most of his time working on music in the studio, and it remains a sacred place for him.

    His recording studio is a fortress with a massive parking lot where his classic cars reside. For a sports fan, there is ample space to play basketball and an indoor game room. As mentioned by the LA Times, the studio has a soundstage and three recording studios adorned with portraits and photographs of family and friends. Two recording rooms in the studio are called Mothership and Battleship, with the former housing the main workstation. Snoop has exclusively recorded some of his gospel albums in the recording studio and continues using them to create new music.

    Around February 2022, Snoop made another purchase of a duplex home a few miles away from the So-Fi Stadium in Los Angeles for $1.29 million. The property was constructed in 1948 and combined two houses to create a complex with four bedrooms and two bathrooms. Snoop Dogg has only sold one of his properties and continues to live in his Diamond Bar Mansion, which has increased in value over the last two decades.
