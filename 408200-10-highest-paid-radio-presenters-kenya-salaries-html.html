    Top 10 highest-paid radio presenters in Kenya

    Radio is one of the popular broadcasting mechanisms worldwide because of its ease of transmission and connection. This has made a popular platform for the media industry to make a buck entertaining the masses.

    Some listen to the music, but others cannot wait for their favourite presenters in various segments or shows. In Kenya, radio stations have certain unique hosts that have become a staple, mainly in the morning. And their capability of tackling interesting topics and keeping the audience listening earns them a big take-home.

    1. Maina Kageni - Ksh.1.5 to 1.8 million

    Maina Kageni's salary at Classic 105 has for long been shrewd in controversy. The renowned radio presenter earns a whooping estimate of Ksh.1.5 to Ksh.1.8 million. This is exclusive of the bonuses he makes from the station, which probably amounts to millions for his outspoken brand and endorsements.

    The talented radio presenter hosts the Breakfast show on Classic 105 with Daniel Ndambuki, a.k.a. Mwalimu King’ang’i (Churchill). How did he achieve such great success in Kenya’s radio industry and start earning a high salary?

    His journey is fascinating. Many people are not aware that one of the highest-paid radio presenters didn't start his career in the media industry. He once worked as a truck driver in the United Kingdom and later ventured into the automobile export industry.

    After returning to Kenya, he started at Capital FM then went to Nation FM as a swing presenter. He got a lucrative offer from Patrick Quarcoo to work at Kiss FM that agreed to pay him Ksh.380,000, and the rest is history.

    2. Daniel “Churchill” Ndambuki - Ksh. 1.2 million

    Everyone knows Daniel Ndambuki from his comedy enterprise, Laugh Industries, whereby he hosts various comics. He is also a radio host, co-hosting the popular Classic 105 Breakfast show with Maina Kageni.

    His estimated earning every month from his radio hosting gig is Ksh.1.2 million, and this excludes whatever brand endorsements and bonuses he might receive from his employer.

    3. Joseph Ogidi Opiyo (Gidi) - Ksh.1 million

    Numerous people all over Kenya tune in to Radio Jambo in the morning to listen to the popular breakfast show “Patanisho. The show reconnects estranged lovers and even friends live on radio and is hosted by Gidi. He became famous in the early 2000s from the song Unbwogable, featuring Maji Maji.

    Considering the show's tremendous success and wide listenership all over Kenya, Joseph Ogidi must be earning a massive salary every month. One of the best things about the radio presenter is that he has remained relevant in the ever-changing media industry.

    Gidi earns a salary of between Ksh.600,000 and Ksh.1 million and is among the highest-paid radio presenters in Kenya. In 2019, he reportedly snubbed a Ksh.2 million Radio Citizen offer after his employer countered and offered him good terms.

    4. Willy M. Tuva - Ksh.800,000

    Among the top radio presenters in Kenya, Willy M. Tuva is one of the most unique. He is identified as one of the most knowledgeable radio hosts in the country, considering his impressive grasp of Kenya and East Africa's prevailing music industry. How much does he earn?

    He hosts Mambo Mseto on Radio Citizen and Mambo Mseto East Africa (now Mseto Africa) on Citizen TV. Mtuva’s colossal following has allowed him to earn a massive salary of around Ksh.800,000 every month.

    5. Daniel Githinji Mwangi, a.k.a. Mbusi - Ksh.700,000

    Daniel Githinji, popularly known as Mbusi, works for Radio Jambo, hosting an exciting show with a great audience of ordinary folk. Previously, he worked for Ghetto radio, where he earned excellent exposure in hosting a radio show that seems to pay off at his current job.

    Mbusi’s unique hosting style that appeals to many Kenyans has earned him a unique persona among radio presenters, and there is likely no other like him. He makes a salary of approximately Ksh.700,000. Mbusi’s show has been getting excellent ratings over time, and his salary might have increased.

    6. Shaffie Weru - Ksh.682,000

    Shaffie Weru, one of the most prolific radio presenters and amongst the new breeds, is amongst Kenya's highest-paid radio presenters. After being unceremoniously fired from HomeBoyz Radio (under Radio Africa Group), he served his employer with a letter with various demands.

    The letter revealed that he was earning a hefty Ksh.682,000 every month. He claimed one month's salary in place of notice and a 12-month salary for unfair termination compensation.

    Considering his exceptional talent and experience in the radio presentation sphere, he is likely to make a comeback to one of the media stations soon.

    7. Jacob “Ghost” Mulee - Ksh.600,000

    Ghost Mulee, the famous coach who is currently coaching the national team, is a radio host. He co-hosts the Patanisho show with Joseph Ogidi Opiyo (Gidi) in the morning, between 6:00 am and 10:00 am on weekdays. He earns an estimated salary of around Ksh.600,000 per month.

    8. Felix Odiwour (Jalang’o) - Ksh.400,000

    According to numerous sources, the famous comedian and radio host currently working at Kiss FM, Jalang’o, earns a hefty amount. He is considered the highest-paid media personality in Kenya with various sources of income considering his amazing popularity. How does he earn?

    He officially joined Kiss FM from Milele FM, where he was working alongside Alex Mwakideu. They had become a famous duo in the radio station, massively increasing viewership over time.

    At Kiss FM, Jalang’o earns approximately Ksh.400,000 every month. Although he has rubbished the rumours of earning a high figure, considering the numerous offers he received after leaving Milele FM, he likely settled at Kiss FM after getting a good deal.

    According to Jalang’o, his YouTube channel earned him Ksh.1.8 million in the first three months from advertising revenue. On top of that, his Instagram channel earns approximately Ksh.3 million every month.

    9. Alex Mwakideu - Ksh. 400,000

    Alex Mwakideu is a talented radio host that made an excellent name for himself at Radio Maisha, where he co-hosted the morning show with Jalango’s. Their fantastic chemistry increased the viewer's numbers at the radio station. However, he left for Mille FM, where he is working until today. How much does he make monthly?

    In 2018, Alex Mwakideu was earning a great six-figure salary. Of course, the amount has increased considering the excellent command of his audience and expanding demand. He is probably making Ksh.100,000 or Ksh.200,000 more than his 2018 salary.

    10. Kamene Goro - Ksh.150,000

    Kamene Goro also has one of the highest radio presenters salaries in Kenya as she hosts a popular show on Kiss FM. The show is one of the most significant for the radio station, whereby she shares the airwaves with the popular Jalang’o.

    Kamene earns an approximate salary of Ksh.150,000 every month less bonuses and brand deals that she might have entered with the media company. And since her numbers are likely growing from when she debuted, her salary might have increased.
